Artificial Intelligence and Art:

There seems to be an opportunity to use AI to reduce the barriers of creativity. General Adversial Networks (GANs) are the structures enabling AI to create fully unique images from a sample dataset.

GANs have two parts to them: the forger, and the detective. The forger will not stop creating images until they create an image so perfect, it outwits the detective. This process enables AI to make completely original & creative art.

CryptoBot Q&A:

What metrics does it use?

After selecting coins based on their Profit Potential (max - current), the bot screens the coins using a moving average convergence/divergence (MACD) that looks at a crypto's price changes over time.


Does the bot require monitoring and control?

While deciding on how to configure your bot, it is important to keep track of what your bot is doing. I will test a bot strategy and send the results to my phone through Discord's API, and make changes as I see fit.


How does the bot decide when to hold/buy?

This is decided by the user, depending on which configuration they pursue. With my current configuration, this bot looks at the coins that are 60% below their 4 day-maximum, and purchase coins two at a time. I am continuing to backtest other options to see which best handles the fast-paced changes that occur 24/7 on the exchange.


CryptoBot Q&A:

Is it successful?

Depends on the market! Using momentum indicators, the bot is suceptable to make some mistakes, but as long as the configuration is reasonable, the bot can continue to make decisions consistent with the market over time.

To guage this, I index my bot against BTC to see how it is doing. I am still new to using the bot, but want to compare its abilities to other similar cryptocurrency bots, and continue to look for innovation in this unique space! :)

Problem 2:

How much does a manufacturing company incur in just boxes?

Solution:

Companies can incur a significant amount of overhead from letting go of the "fine print". For us, it was the shipping costs on our boxes. At times, this cost was so large that it made the boxes unreasonable to use.


So, we went to our box provider to address our concerns. Doing so was a rookie mistake: I handed their competitive advantage (shipping) to them on a silver platter.


Rather than reduce our prices, they effectively raised them as the price of paper rose internationally. This would compound the problems I aimed to solve.

In order to solve this, I had to come up with a way to reduce the cost of shipping on our boxes. I decided the best way was to use local box companies rather than international ones. I asked the warehouses which companies they knew, and composed a small-scale implementation strategy to reduce prices if Rack-a-Tiers needed to in the near-future.

Problem 3:

How can a manufacturing company keep track of duty fees?

Solution:

This was a tricky one. In order to properly account for these costs, there needed to be a system in place to create an ongoing database for this activity.


Since the government relies on an archaic system of fax machines to send duty fees to companies, our "database" was physical. So, I used the digital copies of these PDFs and Google's API (along with Python) to create a system to create a historical log of our duty fees.


Every product and every tariff code has a unique ID. These were the keys I manually entered in from the documents. Then, using the Google Sheets API, I would let my code fill in the rest of the info based on those two entries, which simplified data entry:
